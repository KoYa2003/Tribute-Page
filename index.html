About The Mahatma Gandhi

1869-1948

Mahatma Gandhi, the father of the Indian nation, dedicated his life to nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience. His principles of truth, nonviolence, and peace continue to inspire people worldwide.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others." - Mahatma Gandhi

Gandhi led India to independence from British rule, advocating for the rights of the oppressed and marginalized. His belief in the power of love and compassion transformed societies and paved the way for freedom and equality.

Through his simplicity, humility, and unwavering commitment to justice, Mahatma Gandhi remains an enduring symbol of hope and change. His teachings of tolerance and understanding resonate in the hearts of millions, guiding humanity towards a brighter future.
